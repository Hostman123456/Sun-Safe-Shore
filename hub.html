Melanoma

-a type of skin cancer that develops when melanocytes(cells that give skin melanin) start to grow out of control.
-These cells are in the deep layer of the epidermis. This skin cancer is dangerous because of its ability to spread to other organs rapidly if left unchecked.
-Presents itself as a spot of brown, black, or red color. Remember the ABCDEs of melanoma
  1. Assymetry-the spot is different on each
    2. -Only 20-30% of melanomas are found in existing moles. While 70-80% arise on normal-looking skin. This makes it harder to identify melanoma. Occurs: When skin is exposed to ultraviolet (UV) radiation, it causes skin damage that triggers the melanocytes to produce more melanin, but only the melanin pigment attempts to protect the skin by causing the skin to darken or tan. Melanoma occurs when DNA damage due to UV radiation triggers mutations in the melanocytes, resulting in uncontrolled cellular growth.

Basal Cell Carcinoma

Very common (Around 3 million cases/year) Rolled border/central ulcer/transparent/discoloration Basal cell produce new cell and old one dies. BCC grows slowly and most are curable if detected early. BCC occur due to DNA damages, causing basal cells to change/mutate leading to unremitting growth. 90% are caused by UV radiation Diagnosis and treatment for BCC +77% from 1994-2014 Mohs surgery used often to remove any BCC spots and the layer is later examined to see if any cancerous cells remains
